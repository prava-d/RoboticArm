Forearm

Benjamin Ziemann :: October 24, 2017

As the end of sprint I drew near we realized we needed somewhere to store the electronics to create a more aesthetically pleasing and closer to human image deliverable. We thus decided to create a “forearm” to accomplish this as well as provide a sturdy base.

Going the palm design, we CADed corresponding walls and base to attach to it. Because of the need to house electronics, we made sure to make it hollow as well as include many flat surfaces to provide for mounting of the electronics, along with gaps and small holes to allow for wire management.

To actually fabricate it, we used the laser cutter to cut 3/16” medium density fibreboard in the pattern of CAD drawing sheets.

We then hot glued the pieces together and bolted on the linear actuators. While the Arduino did end up going on the outside if we were to use this as the final design (we will most likely not due to changing palm design) the order in which we put it together would have allowed for it to be completely enclosed.

Further expansion of this forearm will come as we come up with new palm designs and if we decide to take the project further and make a full arm. The addition of a Raspberry Pi to incorporate into the housing will also become a necessity as well. At the very least we will need to have some kind of electronics housing as well as something to lift the gripper to ensure the object grasped will not drop out.
