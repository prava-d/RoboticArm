Pixy Power

Benjamin Ziemann :: November 14, 2017

As object detection was continuing, we decided to take a brief look at some other options for object detection. One of these included the use of the Pixie Camera, a small Arduino and Linux compatible camera that can recognize and threshold for up to 7 colors and dozens of objects in frame within those color thresholds.

After a bit of setup, we got it to work both on Ubuntu and Raspberry Pi. Using Pixymon, the cameraâ€™s GUI, we could see and learn how it was tracking objects. Besides visually tracking objects on the screen, it also provides convenient bounding boxes for each instance of the color, which can then be tagged for tracking. These bounding boxes also each have their associated coordinates which are directly accessible.

Unfortunately while incredible for doing what it advertises it does, this does not quite meet our attempts to identify the material of an object, rather just that it is there or not.
