Teaming

Prava Dhulipalla :: November 15, 2017

So I was sitting in QEA, doing project ideation with two other teams for another overscoped final project that is entirely my fault. As expected, the suggestions I was giving were edging on useless and shameless, much to the excitement of my peers (they didn’t look excited, but I just know). Anyways, when another group had the floor, one of their members said, “We’re a three person team, so we’re also looking into advice and suggestions on dealing with that.”

For some reason I forgot that teaming is, well, vastly important to Olin. It is not simply a means to an end. In PoE, final projects aren’t just to finesse various mechanical, electrical, software, and integration skills. They are also to help one learn to work with a team - as a team.

My PoE team is interesting, because we didn’t actually all choose each other or plan to be together. However, we are all pretty good friends.

I mean on one hand, it makes the more arduous times of the project less daunting because it’s just like you’re hanging out with a group of friends. On the other hand, if this project goes bad, I don’t want seeing my friends’ faces to automatically shook me.

Also, like, have you met me? I’m a little too much even for myself, let alone my friends. And now they have to deal with my antics in PoE? Trust me, even with watered down me in PoE, I still am kind of a wreck (especially in team check-in and team health meetings - those are especially wild). Although I’m excited to work with them, I’m going to kick myself if I ruin their (albeit probably edging on the side of mediocre) opinion of me.

However, this just serves as a catalyst to not let them down. Yeah sure, it sucks if other people think you’re a bad teammate. But if your friends think so - well that’s just downright saddening. Though I’ve already disappointed my parents, my siblings, my extended family, my professors, my roommates, and most of my current/former friends, this particular friend group has experienced minimum disappointment in me. And I shall keep it that way! This is why, when I should be livin’ it up Saturday evening, I’m writing blog posts. A blog post a day keeps their disappointment at bay.
